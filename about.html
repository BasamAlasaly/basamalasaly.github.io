About Me

My journey in computer programming began at a very young age and has grown steadily over the years. In May 2014, two years before graduating from Corona del Sol High School, I earned a Certificate in Computer Programming from Mesa Community College. This early achievement provided me with a strong coding foundation and a significant advantage as I transitioned to higher education. At Arizona State University, I obtained both my B.S. and M.S. in Biomedical Informatics from the College of Health Solutions by May 2021. I further advanced my education by completing the Postbaccalaureate Pre-Professional Program (P4) in Life Sciences from Thomas Jefferson University in 2023. My ultimate aspiration is to become a practicing physician and develop innovative health solutions for the masses.

Currently, I am working as a Clinical Research Coordinator at Dr. Kevin B. Johnson's Artificial Intelligence For Ambulatory Care Innovation Lab at the Perelman School of Medicine at the University of Pennsylvania. As part of the REDUCE project, I am committed to advancing healthcare starting with the improvement of Electronic Health Records. I am excited to contribute to these projects and see their potential impact on the healthcare industry.

With a passion for app development, artificial intelligence, and health solutions, I aim to become a future doctorpreneur. In my free time, I enjoy working out and spending time with friends to maintain an active lifestyle and balance the demands of a career in coding and research.
