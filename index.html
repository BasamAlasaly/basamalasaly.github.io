Arizona State University Applied Project

Leveraging Hyperledger Fabric

The Arizona State University Applied Project was a collaborative blockchain research project for a medical devices company, in which I participated, utilizing Hyperledger Fabric to enhance supply chain management. The project involved detailed research, development, and testing to explore the practical applications of blockchain technology in the industry. We focused on improving device location tracking, managing expiration dates, and streamlining the consignment device reordering process.

Through meetings with corporate representatives and healthcare personnel, I gained valuable insights into the industrial supply chain and helped create efficient workflows to identify key data areas. Our efforts culminated in a successful proof of concept, demonstrating the benefits and capabilities of blockchain technology in real-world scenarios.

LeukApp: Decision Support Web App

React App Usability Testing and Technical Revisions for HonorHealth

LeukApp is a clinical decision support web application designed to assist physicians in the risk assessment and treatment of acute myeloid leukemia. My role in this project involved evaluating LeukApp's usability following third-party development and implementing necessary modifications or enhancements to optimize its functionality. This project was a collaborative research effort between ASU and HonorHealth, aimed at aiding physicians' decision-making and resident training.

I was a presenter for the LeukApp project at the AMIA 2019 Annual Symposium, where it was well received during a productive poster presentation session. Through my user testing with oncologists, fellows, and clinical administrators, I gained valuable insights into the importance of usability and UI/UX testing for ensuring end-user satisfaction and effectiveness in healthcare applications. This experience underscored the critical role of user-centered design in the development of clinical decision support tools.

June, 2020

BankFolio

Fintech iOS Mobile Application for Personal Finance Management

BankFolio is an app I bootstrapped using Apple's native SwiftUI and Swift, leveraging the Plaid API to retrieve and display user bank account information and accompanying transactions in one place. The idea came about when I got charged for subscriptions I forgot I had signed up for and wished there was an easier way to spot those transactions quickly. After discovering Plaid's API and doing some research, I found apps offering this service for an average price of $6 a month, which seemed overpriced. The target audience for this type of application is looking to save money rather than spend it. Therefore, I decided to provide this app to users at a near net operating cost based on their number of connected accounts so more people could take advantage of these features and control their hard-earned money.

In 2022, BankFolio underwent an unsuccessful acquisition that adversely impacted the company's financial health, primarily due to the integration of costly services, such as MX Finance, without corresponding marketing strategies initiation.
